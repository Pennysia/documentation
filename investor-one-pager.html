Investor intro one-pager

Unified Liquidity Infrastructure for DeFi

A unified liquidity layer with two products: an Aggregator of Aggregators exchange for optimal swap execution, and a passive AMM that lets LPs earn directional yield without leverage, liquidation risk, or active position management.

Current snapshot
2 Core products: Exchange and passive liquidity AMM.
1st Place winner at the Sonic S-Tier Global Hackathon.

Why now

  • DEX liquidity is fragmented; no single aggregator consistently wins across trade sizes and pairs.
  • Passive AMM liquidity remains in demand, but standard V2 yields are often too low.
  • Concentrated liquidity favors active professionals and adds operational complexity for everyday LPs.

What Pennysia does

  • Exchange: queries major DEX aggregators in parallel, compares net execution quality, then routes the best path.
  • AMM: keeps V2-style passive liquidity while adding directional fee earning through bucket-based LP positions.
  • Infrastructure: singleton architecture, multi-token flashloans, ERC-6909 LP tokens, and cbrtTWAP oracle design.

Product edge

Aggregator of Aggregators Broadcasts swap requests to aggregators simultaneously and optimizes for output, gas, and execution reliability.
Liquidity Prediction Market LPs choose bullish X, bullish Y, or neutral. Correct directional flow earns taker fees; wrong bias means fewer fees, not added principal loss.
Passive capital efficiency AMM positions auto-compound across trading fees, withdrawal fees, bucket-switch fees, and Aave lending interest.

Business model

Pennysia monetizes AMM activity through protocol fees, with 20% of AMM taker, maker, and liquidity-switch fees allocated to treasury. The Exchange is currently free, with a future swap fee planned below major exchanges and aggregators. Pool deployers can receive up to 50% of protocol fees from markets they bootstrap.

Roadmap

Production stage runs May to September 2026: public testnet, audit, UX refinement, scalable infrastructure, brand/community launch, and mainnet preparation. GTM is targeted for October to December 2026.
